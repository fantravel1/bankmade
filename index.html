📅 History 📅

A Timeline of Capital Power

Key milestones in the history of women shaping global finance, from the first NYSE seat to leading the world's central banks.

1967

Muriel Siebert Breaks the NYSE Barrier

Muriel "Mickie" Siebert becomes the first woman to purchase a seat on the New York Stock Exchange, breaking a 175-year-old barrier in American finance.

1966

Geraldine Weiss Pioneers Dividend Strategy

Geraldine Weiss begins publishing Investment Quality Trends, pioneering the dividend value strategy. She had to use initials to hide her gender from a skeptical industry.

1991

Ariel Investments: Hobson Joins the Mission

Mellody Hobson joins Ariel Investments and begins her ascent to become one of the most prominent value investors and financial literacy advocates in America.

2002

Ho Ching Takes Temasek's Helm

Ho Ching becomes CEO of Temasek Holdings, Singapore's sovereign wealth fund managing hundreds of billions in assets, becoming one of the most powerful capital allocators in Asia.

2014

Janet Yellen Chairs the Federal Reserve

Janet Yellen is sworn in as the first female Chair of the Federal Reserve, becoming the most powerful central banker in the world and steering U.S. monetary policy through critical years of recovery.

2014

Abigail Johnson Ascends at Fidelity

Abigail Johnson becomes CEO of Fidelity Investments, taking command of one of the world's largest asset management firms with trillions in assets under management.

2019

Lagarde Takes the ECB Presidency

Christine Lagarde becomes the first woman to serve as President of the European Central Bank, directing monetary policy for the entire Eurozone after leading the IMF.

2021

Yellen Becomes Treasury Secretary

Janet Yellen is confirmed as the first female U.S. Secretary of the Treasury, becoming the only person in history to have led Treasury, the Fed, and the Council of Economic Advisers.
